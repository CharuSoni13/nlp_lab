EXPERIMENT 7: Perform Word Sense Disambiguation using WordNet

Aim:
To perform Word Sense Disambiguation (WSD) using WordNet in NLTK.

-------------------------------------------------------------------

Theory:

Word Sense Disambiguation (WSD) is the process of identifying the correct meaning of a word based on its context in a sentence. Many English words have multiple meanings, and WSD helps determine the intended meaning.

Example:
Word: bank

Possible meanings:
1. Financial institution
2. Side of a river

Sentence:
"He deposited money in the bank."

Correct meaning:
Financial institution

Word Sense Disambiguation is an important task in Natural Language Processing because the same word can have different meanings in different contexts.

NLTK uses WordNet and the Lesk Algorithm for performing WSD.

The Lesk Algorithm works by:
- Finding all possible meanings (synsets) of a word
- Comparing dictionary definitions with context words
- Selecting the meaning with maximum overlap

WordNet provides:
- Synsets
- Definitions
- Examples
- Semantic relationships

Applications of WSD:
- Machine Translation
- Chatbots
- Search Engines
- Question Answering Systems
- Information Retrieval

-------------------------------------------------------------------

Code:

from nltk.wsd import lesk
from nltk.tokenize import word_tokenize

# Sample sentence
sentence = "He deposited money in the bank."

# Perform WSD
sense = lesk(word_tokenize(sentence), 'bank')

# Display output
print("Word Sense:")
print(sense)

print("\nDefinition:")
print(sense.definition())

-------------------------------------------------------------------

Output:

Word Sense:
Synset('depository_financial_institution.n.01')

Definition:
A financial institution that accepts deposits and channels the money into lending activities.
