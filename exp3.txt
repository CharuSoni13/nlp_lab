Experiment 3: Perform Text Tokenization using NLTK
Aim
To perform text tokenization using the NLTK library in Python.

Theory
Introduction to Tokenization
Tokenization is the process of breaking a large text into smaller units called tokens. Tokens can be:


Words


Sentences


Characters


Tokenization is the first step in most Natural Language Processing (NLP) tasks.
Example:
Sentence:
Natural Language Processing is amazing.
Word Tokens:
['Natural', 'Language', 'Processing', 'is', 'amazing', '.']

Types of Tokenization
1. Word Tokenization
Splits text into individual words.
Example:
I love NLP.
Output:
['I', 'love', 'NLP', '.']

2. Sentence Tokenization
Splits paragraph into individual sentences.
Example:
Hello world. Welcome to NLP.
Output:
['Hello world.', 'Welcome to NLP.']

NLTK for Tokenization
Natural Language Toolkit provides built-in tokenizers for:


Word tokenization


Sentence tokenization


Regular expression tokenization


NLTK uses the Punkt Tokenizer Model for sentence splitting.
